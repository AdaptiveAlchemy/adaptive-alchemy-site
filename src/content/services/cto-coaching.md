## The problem

The jump from senior engineer or engineering manager to CTO is one of the hardest transitions in technology. The skills that got you here — technical depth, hands-on problem solving, good judgment on hard problems — remain valuable. But the job changes fundamentally when you become responsible for what an entire organization produces, not just what you personally build.

Suddenly you are responsible for board communication, product strategy, hiring, vendor decisions, budget, organizational design, and increasingly, product direction alongside technology execution. Most people who have made this transition will tell you the hardest parts were the ones nobody warned them about.

## What you get

A confidential, one-on-one working relationship with a product and technology executive who has held the roles you are navigating:

- **Product and technology strategy** — Roadmap prioritization, product-market fit, architecture choices, build-vs-buy trade-offs
- **Board communication coaching** — How to present product and technology strategy so that boards understand and trust it
- **Leadership development** — Delegation, team structure, managing managers, creating accountability across product and engineering
- **Product-engineering alignment** — How to own both functions without becoming a bottleneck
- **Difficult conversation preparation** — Performance issues, team conflicts, organizational changes
- **Career development** — Role clarity, professional growth, navigating organizational politics
- **Architecture review facilitation** — A second set of experienced eyes on critical technical decisions
- **Async support** — Email and message availability between sessions for time-sensitive decisions

## Who this is for

- **First-time CTOs** who want to accelerate their learning curve and avoid expensive mistakes
- **Engineering leaders stepping up** — VP Engineering, Head of Engineering, or technical co-founders taking on broader product and technology responsibility
- **CTOs inheriting product ownership** — Increasingly common at growth-stage companies where product and technology leadership converge
- **Experienced CTOs in new contexts** — Moving from startup to scale-up, or enterprise to growth-stage, and need a sounding board for the transition
- **Technical founders** who want to grow into the CTO role rather than hire around themselves

## How it works

1. **Intake conversation** — We understand your situation, your goals, and where you feel the biggest gaps
2. **Regular sessions** — Typically bi-weekly or monthly, 90 minutes each. Structured enough to be productive, flexible enough to address what is most pressing
3. **Between sessions** — Async support for decisions that cannot wait

**Typical engagement:** Ongoing, with a minimum 3-month commitment to build the relationship and see results.
