faq:
  - question: 'What is CTO coaching and how does it differ from executive coaching?'
    answer: 'CTO coaching pairs you with a senior technology leader who has held the CTO role at multiple companies. Unlike executive coaches who teach leadership frameworks, a CTO coach provides practitioner-level guidance on real technical decisions: architecture trade-offs, engineering org design, AI strategy, board communication, and scaling challenges. It is a peer relationship, not a teacher-student dynamic.'
  - question: 'What challenges do first-time CTOs typically face?'
    answer: 'First-time CTOs commonly struggle with five transitions: from individual contributor to organizational leader, from technical decisions to business-aligned strategy, from managing engineers to communicating with boards and investors, from building features to building teams and processes, and from being the expert to empowering others to make decisions. A senior CTO advisor accelerates these transitions.'
  - question: 'How do CTOs build a credible AI strategy for their organization?'
    answer: 'Start by separating hype from applicable technology for your specific business. Identify two or three use cases where AI can measurably improve existing workflows, evaluate your data readiness honestly, and build a phased roadmap with quick wins in the first 90 days. Avoid the trap of building an AI team in isolation. Embed AI capability within existing engineering teams for faster adoption.'
  - question: 'When should a CTO seek external senior support?'
    answer: 'Seek external support when you are making high-stakes decisions without a senior peer to pressure-test them, when your organization is scaling faster than your experience covers, when the board is asking for strategy you have not delivered before, or when you need a confidential sounding board for challenges you cannot discuss with your CEO or team.'
  - question: 'How does CTO advisory help with engineering team scaling?'
    answer: 'CTO advisory provides pattern-matched guidance on the organizational challenges that accompany growth: team structure design for your stage, hiring processes that identify strong talent, engineering career ladders, performance management, on-call and incident processes, and the cultural shifts needed as teams grow from 5 to 50 and beyond. These are learned skills, and an advisor shortens the learning curve.'
