# Offering Expansion: Capturing Lower-Market AI Readiness Opportunity

## Context

Adaptive Alchemy is seeing increasing inbound from companies that are stuck at the "beyond ChatGPT, but don't know where to go next" stage. These are not the enterprise boards and PE/VC buyers who are the current primary ICP — but they represent a real, addressable segment that the current offering portfolio doesn't have a clean entry point for.

The risk: capturing them in an ad-hoc way dilutes the premium brand. The opportunity: a structured entry product creates a funded funnel into the full engagement ladder.

## Current State

The engagement ladder (Assess → Design → Pilot → Transform → Operate) already maps the right journey for this buyer. The problem is access: the Assess tier currently lives inside a larger bespoke engagement conversation with no standalone entry point or price signal.

The `/for/stalled-ai-projects` persona page exists and already targets this buyer, but it likely leads into the full transformation narrative rather than a bounded starting offer.

## The Structural Move: Productize the Assess Tier

The cleanest solution is to make the Assess tier purchasable as a standalone, fixed-scope product — not a custom engagement, not a discovery call, but a defined deliverable with a defined price.

### Proposed format

- **Duration:** 2–3 week sprint
- **Input:** Intake interviews + async diagnostic questionnaire
- **Output:** "AI Readiness & Opportunity Map" — a ranked list of use cases with effort/ROI framing, plus a sequenced 90-day action plan
- **Price range:** ~$8–15K (accessible but not cheap — screens tire-kickers, signals expertise)
- **Natural exit:** The roadmap deliverable names the next step, making Design → Pilot a natural follow-on rather than an explicit upsell

### Why this protects the premium

- Premium buyers (boards, PE/VC) don't perceive this as a "lite" offering — they see it as the right starting point before committing to transformation
- The senior engagement services remain bespoke and ladder-priced; this product is bounded and fixed
- It doesn't create a discount dynamic — it creates an on-ramp dynamic

## Positioning

The "Stalled AI Projects" persona page (`/for/stalled-ai-projects`) is the natural landing page. Messaging there should lead with clarity-first framing:

> "Before you know where you're going, you need to know where you actually are."

The offer headline should emphasize a concrete outcome ("a prioritized roadmap you can execute on Monday") over process descriptions.

## What to Avoid

- **Don't create a "lite" transformation service** — that dilutes the flagship
- **Don't build a course or training product** — changes cost structure and brand positioning (already deferred)
- **Don't sub-brand it** — a separate brand creates fragmentation and doubles the marketing surface area

## Open Questions

1. **Productization readiness:** The Assess offering is considered repeatable enough to productize — but the intake and diagnostic components should be documented as a repeatable process before the offer is published.
2. **Pricing validation:** The $8–15K range is a hypothesis. A few early conversations at this price point will confirm whether the segment can absorb it or needs adjustment.
3. **Lead channel:** This buyer arrives via SEO and content (not referral), so the `/for/stalled-ai-projects` page and supporting blog content need to be the primary acquisition surface.

## Next Steps (not yet scoped)

- Document the repeatable Assess process (intake template, diagnostic framework, deliverable structure)
- Update `/for/stalled-ai-projects` page messaging to lead with Assess-as-product
- Determine whether a new `/services/ai-readiness-assessment` page is warranted, or whether this lives under the existing Technology Strategy Assessment page
- Consider a free "AI Readiness Scorecard" as a lead magnet above the paid product
